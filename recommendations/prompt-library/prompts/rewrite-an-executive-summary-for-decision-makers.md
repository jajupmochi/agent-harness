# Rewrite an executive summary for decision makers

> Use for the summary section of a grant application, investor deck or proposal, especially when a previous draft read like a technical abstract.

## Original

```text
重写 executive summary，目前这版很糟糕。

其中第一段是我修改过的，不要改，看看有什么需要优化的地方就行，然后保持这个写作风格。第三段的细节完全没必要 —— 注意这是 executive summary，是一个总结性质的东西，要给评委 / 投资人一看就知道是啥、为啥要给我们钱。

它的目标读者通常是：公司高管、项目负责人、投资人、政府或资助机构、时间有限的决策者。

Executive summary 通常包括什么？一般会回答以下几个问题：
- 背景（Background）：为什么要做这个项目？当前存在什么问题？
- 目标（Objective）：希望达到什么目的？
- 主要内容（Key findings / Proposed solution）：做了什么？采用了什么方法？有哪些重要发现？
- 结论（Conclusion）：最重要的结果是什么？
- 建议（Recommendations）：下一步建议怎么做？

注意这是一个纲要，不一定完全按照这个，但逻辑要清晰。特别是我们是从研究到市场的转换：这个如何做的、为啥可以、市场在哪里、有什么期待（市场来源）等等，要符合我们之前讨论和生成的内容。特别地，你可以看看官方表格给的那些提纲和内容，看看他们的逻辑和侧重看重的内容。
```

## Optimized

```text
Rewrite the executive summary.

Preserve: paragraph 1 is mine. Keep its wording and its voice; suggest improvements rather than
applying them, and match that voice in what you write.

Cut: paragraph 3 is too detailed. An executive summary exists so a reader knows in one pass what this
is and why it deserves funding.

Audience: executives, programme leads, investors, funding bodies. Decision makers with little time.

Cover, in a logical order rather than mechanically:
- Background: why this project, and what problem exists today.
- Objective: what it is meant to achieve.
- Key findings or proposed solution: what was done, by what method, and what came out of it.
- Conclusion: the single most important result.
- Recommendation: what should happen next.

The part that must land hardest is the research-to-market transition: how we make it, why it is
credible, where the market is, and what we expect, with the source of that expectation. Stay
consistent with what we already wrote elsewhere.

Before writing, read the official form's own prompts and structure, and mirror what THEY emphasize.
```

## When to use

Use for the summary section of a grant application, investor deck or proposal, especially when a
previous draft read like a technical abstract.

- The reader decides on this section alone and may not read further.
- Parts of the text are already final and must survive the rewrite untouched, which the preserve
  instruction protects.
- A funder's own form defines what they weigh, and mirroring it is worth more than a generic structure.

## When NOT to use

Do not use it for a technical abstract or a paper. Those reward precision and completeness; this
rewrites toward decision-making, which reads as vague in an academic venue.

Do not use it before the underlying content exists. A summary of an undecided plan produces confident
filler.

Do not use it when you cannot state the market or funding rationale yet. That is the section it
pushes hardest on, and it will be invented if you leave it open.

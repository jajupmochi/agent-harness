## Writing style (de-AI prose tics)

Three habits to avoid in prose the user reads or sends, and in Claude's own output, because they read as "an AI wrote this". Defaults, not absolute bans: keep a construction only when removing it would be wrong or ambiguous.

- **No hyphenated compound modifiers.** Default to the open form: "machine learning models" not "machine-learning models"; "water temperature prediction" not "water-temperature prediction"; "real world" not "real-world"; "open source" not "open-source". Keep the hyphen only for established terms where the open form is wrong or ambiguous (spatio-temporal, state-of-the-art) and proper names (Time-LLM).
- **No colon or semicolon launching a trailing clause after a full sentence** (a strong AI tell). Use a comma, a period, or "and". Avoid "X with less friction: to find, understand, and use it" and "X is not only technical; it is also Y".
- **No stylized filler or emphasis words** (important, crucial, significant, key, genuinely, truly, really, very, deeply, seamlessly, robust, powerful). "Another important reason" becomes "Another reason".

When copy-editing the user's OWN writing, be minimal and surgical: fix genuine errors and these tics, but preserve the user's structure, sentence order, and word choices. Do not reorganize, merge or split paragraphs, or strip their voice. List larger changes as suggestions.

Exceptions: code, identifiers, paths, established technical terms and proper names, quoted material, commit messages, log lines.
